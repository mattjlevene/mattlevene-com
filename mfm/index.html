Top 3 Growth Strategies

Q: If we hired you right now and said "grow the show", what are the three most impactful things you'd do?

1

Amplify Distribution

Increase production and consistency across every platform.

You already have workflows for distributing content, but there's room to increase production and consistency across platforms. Here's what I'd focus on:

  • Create 4-5 clips from each episode for YT, X, IG, FB, TikTok, and LinkedIn.
  • Create 1-2 custom clips or written posts for Shaan, Sam, and guest that matches the vibe of their social profiles.
  • Continually repost and mix in top-performing clips from the past.
  • Experiment with generating written content from podcasts to distribute as LinkedIn posts, X threads, etc.
2

Double Down on Winners

Double down on the formulas that are already working.

Do more episodes with angles that are already working:

55% of top 40 episodes on YouTube are about getting rich / building wealth with a specific method or story (especially related to AI). There are two formulas that seem to work really well:

0 Employees / Extreme Leverage. 60% of the top 40 episodes have a title with a specific dollar amount + extreme outcome (ideally with a surprising constraint), e.g. "This Guy Makes $20,000,000/Year With 0 Employees." It's aspirational, specific, and slightly unbelievable in a good way.

Personal Transformation / Hero's Journey. Specifically with ages + dollar amounts, e.g "From Broke At 26 To $100 Million Net Worth By 31" or "From High School Dropout To $952 Million At 33."

3

Audience Research & Insights

Get inside the head of our listeners.

Gather audience data to figure out what segments or topics are under-represented. Get inside the head of our listeners. Run surveys and scrape podcast reviews, episode + social media comments, Reddit commentary, etc. Maybe people want to learn more about value investing, ideas in health, or starting businesses in college. I did an initial search by scraping the most recent 2,100 YouTube comments and 500 Apple Podcast reviews, which led to a few insights like these:

Female listeners appear to be a hidden loyal segment. 40+ comments specifically self-identify as female listeners, e.g. "I know I'm not the target demo BUT I love this." Might be an opportunity to bring on more female founders or mix in sharing some trends that appeal to a female audience.

There seems to be an execution gap for a lot of people who listen to the show, but don't take action. One idea could be to create AI prompts / action plans for each episode, which could also double as a lead magnet.

If You Gave Me $50K

Q: If we gave you $50k to do something dope for the MFM community, what would you do?

ZERO TO HERO

I would give away fifty $1,000 startup grants to listeners who successfully complete a 48-hour startup sprint. We could call it Zero to Hero.

1 Apply

Listeners fill out an application and pick an idea from the show to build.

2 Build

They have to get 3 strangers to pay for it within 48 hours.

3 Share

Throughout the sprint, they post updates, wins, and failures on X. This creates a ton of organic content repping MFM.

4 Win

The first 50 people who pull it off each get $1,000. We'll pick one winner to come on the show and break down what happened.
